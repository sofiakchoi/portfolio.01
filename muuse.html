Design for Muuse, a digital research and curation tool drawing from museum archives. The platform aims to provide equitable access to archival materials, using trusted institutions to encourage education and creative cultivation.

The Problem

Muuse: the Solution

The mobile platform allows users to search for pieces and create "collections", personal curations of knowledge.

Users can message, comment, and connect with other users on the platform, generating a personal learning community.

All pieces featured on the platform come with a detailed summary providing context, including important information like copyright status.

The platform offers both a web and mobile app option. The website allows museums to securely sign-in and upload their own materials.

Administrator homepage displays viewer analytics, allowing museums to target content or potentially uplift material not getting enough eyes.

Institutions can create verified "collections" out of their own uploaded archives. These act as digital exhbits, with comments displayed along with the content acting has placards.

Research

In additon to competitive analysis, we conducted qualitative survey's with five participants regarding their experiences with musuems, accessibility, and representation.
